# A/B Test of Gaming Dataset with Python and Tableau

## Business problem
The company rolled out a new in-game monetization feature to a subset of users (Group B), while the rest remained on the old version (Group A).
The goal is to evaluate whether the new feature improves monetization without negatively affecting user engagement.

## Hypotheses

### Primary hypothesis
Users exposed to the new feature (Group B) generate higher revenue compared to the control group (Group A).

### Secondary hypothesis
The new feature increases the proportion of paying users (conversion rate).

### Guardrail hypothesis
The new feature does not negatively impact user activity.

## Define metrics
- **ARPU** (Average Revenue Per User) — total revenue divided by the total number of users in the group.
- **Conversion rate** — share of users who made at least one purchase, out of all users in the group.
- **ARPPU** (Average Revenue Per Paying User) — total revenue divided by the number of paying users only.
- **Total Revenue** — sum of all revenue generated by the group.

## Insights

1. **ARPU** in the test group (B) is **$24 lower** than in the control group (A), suggesting a decline in average revenue per user. However, this metric alone doesn't explain *why* — it needs to be broken down further.

2. **Conversion rate** also dropped, indicating reduced interest in the new monetization feature:
   - Group A: 0.011432
   - Group B: 0.008278

3. **ARPPU**, however, *increased* despite the decline in the metrics above — paying users in group B placed more orders on average:
   - Group A: $18,672.07
   - Group B: $22,919.29

4. 4. To confirm whether the difference in conversion rate is statistically significant, a **z-test for proportions** was conducted.
   The test returned **p-value = 0.03**, which is below the standard significance threshold (α = 0.05).
   This means the difference in conversion rate between groups A and B is **statistically significant** — it's unlikely to be due to random chance.

## Conclusion
- The **primary hypothesis is not confirmed**: ARPU decreased in group B instead of increasing.
- The **secondary hypothesis is not confirmed**: conversion rate decreased in group B, and the z-test (p = 0.03) confirms this drop is statistically significant, not random noise.
- However, ARPPU increased significantly — among users who did convert, the new feature monetized them more effectively.
- Overall, the new feature **reduces the number of paying users but increases revenue per paying user**. The net effect on ARPU is negative, so the feature in its current form should not be rolled out without further changes (e.g. addressing onboarding/pricing friction that may be reducing conversion).
